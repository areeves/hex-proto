Saucy 7 Gamma

On the smallest of the three moons orbiting the seventh planet in the Saucy System live a large population of large bees. Enormous bees. Mind-breakingly large bees. And, where you have lots of mind-breakingly large bees, you have mind-breakingly large amounts of honey. Low-gravity honey. The good stuff.

Where there's money to be made, people flock to cash-in, and the Golden Rush of Saucy 7 Gamma is no exception. Interprising individuals (who hire more expendable individuals, that's you) have tireless sought to eliminate any remaining humans without diabetes. For something done in space, the process is surprisingly uncomplicated- go in, get honey, don't die, leave. The not-dieing is the tricky part as the bees lurk unseen in the large pools of honey. As dangerous as a bridge run on the Shattered Plains.

Fortunately, one lazy worker laid down on the rock at the bottom of an empty honey pit and found he could hear the bees moving around in the adjacent pits. Turns out one accurately determine how many adjacent pits are occupied, just not which ones. Needless to say, it's still dangerous, but brighter people will have a better chance of surival.
